The Perfect Cà Phê Sữa Đá Trail Through Saigon

How to find Saigon's best iced coffee — from 80,000₫ rooftop views to 15,000₫ plastic-stool perfection.

☕ Food & Drink Ho Chi Minh City April 10, 2025 5 min read

By Ana Reyes, Mekong Trails Co-founder

There are few things in this world as perfectly calibrated as a glass of cà phê sữa đá on a sweltering Saigon afternoon. Strong Robusta coffee, drip-brewed through a phin filter into a glass of condensed milk, poured over a mountain of crushed ice. It is simultaneously a caffeine delivery system, a social ritual, and a survival mechanism.

I have drunk an embarrassing number of these across Ho Chi Minh City, and I am here to tell you: not all cà phê sữa đá are equal. Here is your trail.

Stop 1: The Alley Stall (District 1)

Your first stop should cost no more than 15,000–20,000₫. Find a plastic-stool café tucked into an alley off Bùi Viện or Phạm Ngũ Lão. A woman sits behind a rack of phin filters and a giant thermos of condensed milk. The coffee arrives lukewarm, dense, and slightly smoky. It is perfect.

This is the baseline. This is what everything else should be measured against.

"The best cà phê sữa đá I have ever had cost 18,000₫ and was served by a grandmother who has been making it the same way since 1987."

Stop 2: The Phin Purists (District 3)

Head to District 3 for cafés that have elevated the phin to an art form. These spots use single-origin Vietnamese highland beans — typically from Đắk Lắk province — and allow 10 minutes for the drip. The coffee is more complex, with chocolate and fruit notes that cheap Robusta blends lack.

Budget: 40,000–60,000₫. Worth every đồng.

Stop 3: The Rooftop View (Bình Thạnh)

By mid-afternoon, treat yourself to a rooftop café — not for the coffee (it will be worse than the alley), but for the view of the city's skyline and the Saigon River. The price jumps to 70,000–90,000₫, which you are mostly paying for the sunset. That's fair.

The Verdict

The plastic stool wins on flavour and on authenticity. The phin purists win on craft. The rooftop wins on Instagram. You should do all three in one afternoon, which is exactly what this trail maps out.

Quick Reference

  • Alley stall: 15,000–20,000₫ · District 1 alleys
  • Phin purist café: 40,000–60,000₫ · District 3
  • Rooftop: 70,000–90,000₫ · Bình Thạnh
  • Best time: 7–9am (morning session) or 2–4pm (afternoon slump)
  • Avoid: Touristy cafés on Đồng Khởi Street — overpriced and under-caffeinated.

Now go. The city is hot, the day is long, and there is crushed ice waiting for you.
