🇻🇳 Vietnam

Hội An — The Town of Lanterns

Hội An is Vietnam's most charming destination — a well-preserved trading port that has barely changed since the 15th century. Its lantern-lit streets, tailors, and riverside cafés make it endlessly photogenic.

Best time to visit: February – April (dry season, perfect cycling weather).

Don't miss: The monthly Lantern Festival (14th of every lunar month), An Bàng Beach (5km away), and the legendary Mì Quảng noodle soup.

  • UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Best explored by bicycle
  • Budget: 300,000 – 800,000₫/day
  • Recommended stay: 3–5 days

🇻🇳 Vietnam

Sapa — The Misty Highlands

Perched at 1,500m in northern Vietnam's Hoàng Liên Son range, Sapa is the gateway to the country's most dramatic mountain scenery — and to the rich cultures of the Hmong, Dao, and Tày peoples.

Best time to visit: September – November (post-harvest, golden terraces) or March – May (flowers in bloom).

Don't miss: Trekking to Cát Cát village, the Fansipan summit cable car, and the Bắc Hà weekend market.

  • Vietnam's highest peak (Fansipan 3,143m)
  • Book trek guides locally — support the community
  • Budget: 500,000 – 1,200,000₫/day
  • Recommended stay: 3–5 days

🇰🇭 Cambodia

Angkor Wat — Temples of the Gods

No photograph can prepare you for Angkor Wat — the world's largest religious monument. Rise before 5am for the famous reflection shot in the moat, then spend the day exploring the hundreds of temples that make up the Angkor Archaeological Park.

Best time to visit: November – March (cool dry season).

Don't miss: Ta Prohm (the "Tomb Raider" temple), Bayon's 200 serene faces, and a sunset from Phnom Bakheng hill.

  • UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • 3-day pass recommended: US$62
  • Hire a local tuk-tuk driver for the full circuit
  • Recommended stay: 2–4 days

🇱🇦 Laos

Luang Prabang — Soul of Laos

Luang Prabang sits at the confluence of the Mekong and Nam Khan rivers, encircled by jungle-clad mountains. Its 33 gilded temples and colonial-era shophouses create an atmosphere unlike anywhere else in Southeast Asia.

Best time to visit: November – February (cool season).

Don't miss: The monks' alms-giving ceremony at dawn, Kuang Si waterfall, and the night market on Sisavangvong Road.

  • UNESCO World Heritage City
  • Arrive by slow boat from Thailand — unmissable journey
  • Budget: US$25–60/day
  • Recommended stay: 4–6 days
